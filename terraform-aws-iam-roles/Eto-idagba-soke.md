## 3. Universal Progression Rule

> **You must perform 70%+ of the next level’s responsibilities for ≥6 consecutive months before promotion.**

Evidence sources (same for every track):
- Dashboards & metrics
- Peer/Dev 360 feedback
- Delivered projects & artifacts
- Mentoring hours logged

## 5. Promotion to L6 / M6 (both require panel + sponsorship)

**L5 → L6 Principal Architect**
- 12+ months acting as enterprise technical owner
- Authored ≥1 company-wide security standard that is enforced
- External proof (talk, blog, open-source, or internal tool used by 3+ orgs)
- Unanimous L6+ panel approval

**L5 → M6 Manager**
- 12+ months managing ≥6 people (or matrix)
- Team ≥90th-percentile engagement score
- Delivered major program on-time/under-budget
- Built succession (≥1 direct report promoted)
- VP + HRBP approval

Security Team Career Path Framework

For Security Analysts, Engineers, and Architects

⸻

1. Role Definitions

Purpose

Provide clear, consistent expectations across the entire security engineering ecosystem, preventing disparities in compensation, responsibility, and growth trajectories.

⸻

### Intern / Security Intern

Role Purpose:
Support operational tasks while learning foundational security competencies.

Core Responsibilities
	•	Execute low-risk operational tasks under close supervision.
	•	Assist with documentation, scanning, and data preparation.
	•	Shadow analysts to understand investigations and tooling.

Skills & Expectations
	•	Basic understanding of cybersecurity fundamentals.
	•	Willingness to learn and receive coaching.
	•	No ownership of complex tasks.

⸻

### Junior Analyst

Role Purpose:
Contribute to foundational security operations with guidance.

Core Responsibilities
	•	Execute routine tasks: triage alerts, run vulnerability scans, prepare reports.
	•	Follow documented processes precisely.
	•	Escalate issues appropriately.

Skills & Expectations
	•	Understand core security concepts (IAM, SIEM, EDR, networking basics).
	•	Write clear, concise updates.
	•	Reliably execute tasks with moderate support.

⸻

### Security Analyst

Role Purpose:
Handle standard operational responsibilities independently.

Core Responsibilities
	•	Lead initial triage and validation of security events.
	•	Conduct vulnerability analysis and coordinate remediation.
	•	Manage technical queues (tickets, alerts, requests).
	•	Support compliance, audit, and asset discovery efforts.

Skills & Expectations
	•	Strong foundational knowledge across cloud, network, IAM, endpoint, and DevSecOps areas.
	•	Ability to independently investigate issues and propose remediation steps.
	•	Consistent accuracy, reliability, and communication.

⸻

### Security Engineer

Role Purpose:
Design, deploy, and optimize security tools, controls, and automation.

Core Responsibilities
	•	Engineer detections, integrations, dashboards, pipelines, and guardrails.
	•	Own mid-complexity projects with minimal oversight.
	•	Author technical runbooks, IaC modules, playbooks, and SOPs.
	•	Partner with product/infra teams to improve security posture.

Skills & Expectations
	•	Strong cloud security engineering (AWS/Azure/GCP).
	•	Competence in automation (Python, Terraform, CI/CD).
	•	Independently solves technical problems and drives improvements.
	•	Demonstrates early leadership capabilities.

⸻

### Senior Security Engineer

Role Purpose:
Operate as technical leader, mentor, and domain expert.

Core Responsibilities
	•	Architect and implement high-impact security capabilities.
	•	Lead complex projects spanning multiple teams.
	•	Mentor analysts and engineers.
	•	Drive strategic improvements, propose new guardrails and standards.
	•	Act as escalation for complex investigations or incidents.

Skills & Expectations
	•	Advanced knowledge across multiple domains (IAM, cloud, detection, DevSecOps, AppSec).
	•	Highly autonomous decision-making.
	•	Strong documentation, influencing, and communication skills.
	•	Demonstrates ability to lead without authority.

⸻

### Security Architect

Role Purpose:
Define strategic security direction, architecture patterns, and enterprise guardrails.

Core Responsibilities
	•	Set global security principles, architecture patterns, and reference designs.
	•	Partner with Engineering, IT, and Product leadership to drive secure designs.
	•	Evaluate emerging technologies, perform threat modeling, and define control baselines.
	•	Provide technical oversight to engineering teams.
	•	Represent security in roadmap decisions.

Skills & Expectations
	•	Deep multi-cloud architecture expertise.
	•	Ability to translate business goals into scalable security capabilities.
	•	Executive communication, influence, and cross-org leadership.
	•	Operates with full autonomy and accountability.

⸻

2. Progression Criteria

Foundational Principles
	•	Advancement is based on demonstrated capability, not tenure.
	•	Individuals must consistently perform at the next level before promotion.
	•	Metrics must be measurable, repeatable, and objective.

⸻

Promotion Criteria by Area

Technical Competency
	•	Mastery of required skills for the next level.
	•	Competent in core tooling for role (SIEM, CSPM, IAM, EDR, cloud services).
	•	Evidence of autonomy in troubleshooting and solution design.

Execution & Delivery
	•	Demonstrated ability to deliver defined work with quality.
	•	Proactive ownership of tasks and projects.
	•	Clear reduction of dependency on higher-level staff.

Communication
	•	Clear, structured communication in tickets, design docs, and meetings.
	•	Proactive stakeholder engagement.
	•	Ability to articulate risks and remediation paths.

Leadership & Initiative
	•	Coaches peers; contributes to improving processes.
	•	Drives small → mid → large projects (depending on level).
	•	Demonstrates judgment, ownership, and team-aligned decisions.

Cultural Contribution
	•	Contributes positively to team environment.
	•	Supports cross-team collaboration.
	•	Embodies the organization’s security values.

3. Skill Development Pathways

Core Skill Areas

Level	Required Skills
Intern	Basic cybersecurity fundamentals
Junior Analyst	SIEM basics, vuln scanning, IAM fundamentals, ticket quality
Analyst	Cloud basics, EDR, detection, workflow ownership, automation fundamentals
Engineer	Cloud engineering, scripting, automation, API/Terraform, detection development
Senior Engineer	Domain expertise, architecture-level thinking, cross-team leadership
Architect	Strategic multi-cloud architecture, governance, threat modeling, system design


⸻

Recommended Training & Certifications

Early Career (Intern → Analyst)
	•	CompTIA Security+
	•	Google Cybersecurity Certificate
	•	Splunk Core User
	•	AWS/Azure/GCP Fundamentals

Mid Career (Engineer → Senior Engineer)
	•	AWS Security Specialty
	•	Azure Security Engineer
	•	GCP Professional Cloud Security Engineer
	•	OSCP (optional but differentiating)
	•	GIAC Cloud Security (GCLD, GCSA, GMOB)
	•	HashiCorp Terraform Associate
	•	Splunk Power User / ES Analyst

Advanced Career (Senior Engineer → Architect)
	•	GIAC Cloud Architect
	•	AWS Solutions Architect Professional
	•	Azure Architect Technologies
	•	CISSP / CCSP
	•	SABSA or TOGAF (for architecture governance)

⸻

Knowledge Transfer Mechanisms
	•	Monthly “Security Engineering Deep Dives.”
	•	Pair programming/peer reviews for IaC and automation tasks.
	•	Shadowing senior engineers during incident response.
	•	Cross-team rotations (CloudOps, DevOps, Networking, Platform).

4. Performance Expectations

Key Indicators

Category	Expectations

Information Accuracy	Submissions must be technically correct, validated, and peer-reviewed
Communication Effectiveness	Documentation clarity, meeting updates, stakeholder alignment
Team Collaboration	Participates in reviews, supports peers, shares context
Initiative	Identifies and resolves gaps without being asked
Problem-Solving	Analytical thinking, root-cause analysis, solution engineering

Accountability Framework
	•	Quarterly performance check-ins.
	•	Project retrospectives to capture lessons learned.
	•	Clear ownership mapped via RACI for major initiatives.
	•	Documented improvement plans when expectations are not met.

5. Mentorship & Growth Framework

Structured Mentorship
	•	Junior roles paired with Senior Engineers or Architects.
	•	1:1 bi-weekly coaching sessions.
	•	Career development goal-setting every 6 months.

Knowledge Transfer
	•	Scheduled “tech-share” sessions.
	•	Architecture walkthroughs led by senior staff.
	•	Rotational exposure to different security domains.

Confidence & Leadership Development
	•	Encourage junior team members to present findings.
	•	Assign lead roles on low-risk projects to build ownership.
	•	Continuous feedback loop focusing on strengths and opportunities.

⸻

6. Flexibility & Review Mechanism

Principles
	•	The framework must evolve with technology, organizational maturity, and team structure.
	•	Growth paths must support individual strengths while maintaining organizational consistency.
	•	Exceptions may be approved for high performers who demonstrate capability earlier.

Review Cycles
	•	Annual formal review of career path documentation.
	•	Mid-year checkpoint to adjust expectations based on technology or org changes.
	•	Feedback-driven updates collected from employees anonymously.

Outcome Goal

A supportive, transparent, and fair structure that:
	•	Promotes talent equitably
	•	Retains high performers
	•	Encourages continuous learning
	•	Scales with organizational growth
